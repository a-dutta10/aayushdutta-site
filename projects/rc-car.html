RC Car — Wheels Manufacturing at Scale

A manufacturing-driven RC car build focused on producing a full wheel system at scale and optimizing traction and robustness for obstacle-course performance. We placed 1st out of 6 teams with a 42s course time and produced 160 wheels.

PRIMARY OBJECTIVES
Manufacturing at scale · Traverse obstacle course
DESIGN GOALS
Traverse obstacles · Speed · Traction · DFM · System compatibility
SCOPE
Wheel · Tire · Hub · Fasteners
VOLUME
160 wheels manufactured

OVERVIEW

This project emphasized building a wheel system that could be manufactured repeatably and integrated cleanly with drivetrain, suspension, and steering constraints. We iterated the wheel and tire design for performance on the obstacle course while optimizing the production process for throughput and reliability.

WHAT WE BUILT

  • Aluminum wheel geometry optimized for stiffness-to-weight and manufacturability.
  • Cast urethane tire selected for traction and durability.
  • Mounting hub and fastening system for repeatable assembly.

MANUFACTURING PROCESS

We developed a full production workflow: waterjet blanks → lathe jig and fixturing → CAM finishing. The lathe strategy was revised to reduce cycle time significantly while maintaining surface quality.

Production was scaled and validated, with fixtures and process flow optimized for speed, repeatability, and consistent output across all 160 wheels.

RESULTS

  • 1st place out of 6 teams (42 seconds).
  • 160 wheels manufactured at scale.
  • Hands-on experience in fixture design, CAM, process optimization, and scaled production under deadline.
